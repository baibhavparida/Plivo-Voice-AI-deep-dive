## Core Technologies

### ASR (Automatic Speech Recognition)
Technology that converts spoken language into written text. ASR systems analyze audio input, identify speech patterns, and transcribe them into machine-readable text format. ASR is the foundational input technology for voice AI systems, enabling machines to understand human speech.

**Related Terms:** STT, Speech-to-Text, Transcription, Voice Recognition

### STT (Speech-to-Text)
The process of converting spoken audio into written text. Often used interchangeably with ASR, though STT typically refers to the output process while ASR encompasses the entire recognition system.

**Related Terms:** ASR, Transcription, Dictation

### TTS (Text-to-Speech)
Technology that converts written text into synthetic spoken audio. Modern TTS systems use neural networks to produce natural-sounding speech with appropriate prosody, emotion, and intonation.

**Related Terms:** Speech Synthesis, Voice Synthesis, Neural TTS

### VAD (Voice Activity Detection)
Algorithm that detects the presence or absence of human speech in an audio signal. VAD distinguishes speech segments from silence, background noise, and non-speech sounds. Critical for efficient voice processing.

**Related Terms:** Speech Detection, Endpointing, Silence Detection

### NLU (Natural Language Understanding)
A subset of NLP focused on machine comprehension of human language meaning. NLU extracts semantic meaning, intent, and entities from text or transcribed speech.

**Related Terms:** Intent Recognition, Entity Extraction, Semantic Analysis, NLP

### NLP (Natural Language Processing)
A field of AI dealing with the interaction between computers and human language. NLP encompasses both understanding (NLU) and generation (NLG) of natural language.

**Related Terms:** NLU, NLG, Computational Linguistics, Text Processing

### NLG (Natural Language Generation)
Technology that produces human-readable text from structured data or semantic representations. NLG creates natural, contextually appropriate responses for voice systems.

**Related Terms:** Text Generation, Response Generation, Language Model

### Wake Word
A specific word or phrase that activates a voice assistant from a standby state (also called Hotword or Trigger Word). Wake words are processed locally using lightweight, always-on models. Examples include "Alexa," "Hey Siri," and "OK Google."

**Related Terms:** Hotword, Trigger Phrase, Keyword Spotting, KWS

### KWS (Keyword Spotting)
Technology for detecting specific words or phrases within continuous speech. KWS systems are optimized for low-latency, low-power operation to enable always-on listening.

**Related Terms:** Wake Word Detection, Hotword Detection, Voice Trigger

### Conversational AI
AI systems designed to engage in human-like dialogue, understanding context, managing multi-turn conversations, and providing relevant responses. Combines NLU, dialogue management, and NLG.

**Related Terms:** Dialogue System, Chatbot, Voice Assistant, Virtual Agent

### Voice Assistant
An AI-powered software agent that responds to voice commands and performs tasks on behalf of users. Integrates ASR, NLU, dialogue management, and TTS into a cohesive user experience.

**Related Terms:** Virtual Assistant, Digital Assistant, Smart Speaker, Voice Bot

### Voice Bot
An automated conversational agent that interacts with users through voice, typically handling specific tasks or workflows. Commonly deployed in customer service and support scenarios.

**Related Terms:** Voice Agent, Virtual Agent, IVR, Conversational IVR

### Multimodal AI
AI systems that process and integrate information from multiple input types (modalities) such as voice, text, images, and gestures. Provides richer, more contextual interactions.

**Related Terms:** Cross-Modal, Multimedia AI, Multi-Input Systems

## Audio Processing

### Sampling Rate
The number of audio samples captured per second, measured in Hertz (Hz). Common rates: 8kHz (telephony), 16kHz (wideband speech), 44.1kHz (CD quality), 48kHz (professional audio). Voice AI typically uses 16kHz for optimal balance.

**Related Terms:** Nyquist Frequency, Sample Rate, Audio Resolution

### Nyquist Frequency
The highest frequency that can be accurately captured at a given sampling rate, equal to half the sampling rate. To capture a 4kHz signal, you need at least 8kHz sampling.

**Related Terms:** Sampling Rate, Aliasing, Bandwidth

### MFCC (Mel-Frequency Cepstral Coefficients)
A representation of audio that mimics human auditory perception. MFCCs compress spectral information into a compact feature vector commonly used as input to speech recognition systems.

**Related Terms:** Mel Spectrogram, Feature Extraction, Cepstrum

### Mel Spectrogram
A visual representation of audio frequency content over time, with frequencies mapped to the mel scale (which approximates human pitch perception). Common input to neural speech models.

**Related Terms:** Spectrogram, MFCC, Filterbank, Short-Time Fourier Transform

### Filterbank
A set of bandpass filters that decompose an audio signal into multiple frequency bands. Mel filterbanks weight these bands according to human perception.

**Related Terms:** Mel Filterbank, MFCC, Frequency Analysis

### Spectrogram
A visual representation showing how the frequency content of a signal changes over time. X-axis represents time, y-axis represents frequency, and color intensity represents amplitude.

**Related Terms:** Mel Spectrogram, STFT, Time-Frequency Representation

### FFT (Fast Fourier Transform)
An algorithm that efficiently computes the Discrete Fourier Transform, converting a time-domain signal into its frequency components. Fundamental to audio analysis.

**Related Terms:** DFT, STFT, Frequency Analysis, Spectral Analysis

### STFT (Short-Time Fourier Transform)
A technique that divides audio into short overlapping segments and applies FFT to each, producing a time-frequency representation.

**Related Terms:** FFT, Spectrogram, Windowing, Overlap-Add

### Audio Codec
Software or hardware that compresses and decompresses digital audio. Common speech codecs include G.711, G.729, Opus, and Speex.

**Related Terms:** Compression, Encoding, G.711, Opus

### PCM (Pulse Code Modulation)
A method for digitally representing analog audio signals. PCM samples the amplitude of the audio waveform at regular intervals and quantizes each sample to a discrete value. The standard uncompressed audio format.

**Related Terms:** Sampling, Quantization, WAV, Raw Audio

### Bit Depth
The number of bits used to represent each audio sample. Higher bit depth provides greater dynamic range. Common values are 16-bit (96dB range) and 24-bit.

**Related Terms:** Dynamic Range, Quantization, Audio Quality

### Noise Reduction
Signal processing techniques that reduce unwanted background noise while preserving speech. Modern approaches use neural networks trained to separate speech from various noise types.

**Related Terms:** Denoising, Noise Cancellation, Speech Enhancement

### AEC (Acoustic Echo Cancellation)
Technology that removes echo caused by speaker output being picked up by the microphone. Essential for speakerphone applications and full-duplex conversation.

**Related Terms:** Echo Suppression, Full Duplex, Barge-In

### AGC (Automatic Gain Control)
A system that automatically adjusts audio signal amplitude to maintain consistent volume levels regardless of speaker distance or volume.

**Related Terms:** Volume Normalization, Dynamic Range Compression, Level Control

### Diarization
The process of partitioning an audio stream into segments according to speaker identity, answering "who spoke when." Essential for meeting transcription and multi-party analysis.

**Related Terms:** Speaker Segmentation, Speaker Identification, Voice Fingerprinting

### Beamforming
A signal processing technique using multiple microphones to focus on sound from a specific direction while reducing noise from other directions.

**Related Terms:** Microphone Array, Spatial Filtering, Far-Field Recognition

### Far-Field Recognition
Speech recognition optimized for audio captured at a distance (typically 1-5 meters). Requires beamforming, noise reduction, and robust acoustic models.

**Related Terms:** Beamforming, Microphone Array, Room Acoustics

## Machine Learning / AI

### Transformer
A neural network architecture based on self-attention mechanisms, enabling parallel processing of sequential data. Foundation of all modern LLMs and speech models.

**Related Terms:** Self-Attention, BERT, GPT, Encoder-Decoder

### Attention Mechanism
A neural network component that allows models to focus on relevant parts of the input when producing each output. Computes weighted combinations of input elements based on relevance.

**Related Terms:** Self-Attention, Multi-Head Attention, Cross-Attention, Transformer

### LLM (Large Language Model)
Neural networks with billions of parameters trained on vast text corpora, capable of understanding and generating human-like text. Exhibit emergent capabilities including reasoning and instruction-following.

**Related Terms:** GPT, Foundation Model, Generative AI, Language Model

### LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory)
A recurrent neural network architecture designed to capture long-range dependencies in sequential data. Uses gating mechanisms to control information flow through memory cells.

**Related Terms:** RNN, GRU, Sequence Modeling, Recurrent Networks

### RNN (Recurrent Neural Network)
Neural networks designed to process sequential data by maintaining hidden state across time steps. Foundational for early neural speech processing.

**Related Terms:** LSTM, GRU, Sequence-to-Sequence, Hidden State

### CNN (Convolutional Neural Network)
Neural networks using convolutional layers to extract local patterns from data. In speech, CNNs capture spectral and temporal features from audio representations.

**Related Terms:** Convolution, Feature Extraction, Pooling, Convolutional Layers

### Encoder-Decoder
A neural architecture where an encoder processes input into a latent representation, and a decoder generates output from that representation. Enables input-output sequences of different lengths.

**Related Terms:** Sequence-to-Sequence, Attention, Latent Representation

### Fine-Tuning
The process of adapting a pre-trained model to a specific task or domain by continuing training on task-specific data. Leverages knowledge from large-scale pre-training.

**Related Terms:** Transfer Learning, Domain Adaptation, Pre-Training

### Inference
The process of using a trained model to make predictions on new data. Performance (latency, throughput) is critical for real-time voice applications.

**Related Terms:** Prediction, Model Serving, Latency, Throughput

### Embedding
A learned dense vector representation that captures semantic or acoustic properties of input data. Speaker embeddings capture voice characteristics; word embeddings capture semantic meaning.

**Related Terms:** Vector Representation, Latent Space, Feature Vector

### Pre-Training
Initial training phase where models learn general representations from large-scale unlabeled or weakly-labeled data. Creates foundation models for fine-tuning.

**Related Terms:** Self-Supervised Learning, Foundation Model, Transfer Learning

### Quantization
Technique that reduces model precision from 32-bit floating point to lower bit-widths (16-bit, 8-bit, or 4-bit). Reduces memory footprint and accelerates inference.

**Related Terms:** Model Compression, Pruning, Optimization, INT8

### Pruning
Technique that removes unnecessary model parameters to reduce model size and computational requirements. Can be structured or unstructured.

**Related Terms:** Model Compression, Sparsity, Quantization, Distillation

### Knowledge Distillation
Training technique where a smaller "student" model learns to mimic a larger "teacher" model. Transfers knowledge to efficient models suitable for deployment.

**Related Terms:** Model Compression, Teacher-Student, Soft Labels

### Conformer
A hybrid architecture combining convolutional neural networks with transformers, designed specifically for speech processing. Achieves state-of-the-art ASR accuracy.

**Related Terms:** Transformer, CNN, ASR Architecture, Hybrid Model

### Wav2Vec
A self-supervised pre-training framework that learns speech representations from unlabeled audio. Enables high-quality ASR with limited labeled data.

**Related Terms:** Self-Supervised Learning, Pre-Training, HuBERT, Representation Learning

## Speech Recognition

### WER (Word Error Rate)
The primary metric for ASR accuracy: (Substitutions + Insertions + Deletions) / Total Words * 100%. Human transcription WER is approximately 5%. Below 10% is typically production-ready.

**Related Terms:** CER, Accuracy, Substitution Error, Deletion Error

### CER (Character Error Rate)
An accuracy metric calculated at the character level rather than word level. Useful for languages without clear word boundaries.

**Related Terms:** WER, Edit Distance, Levenshtein Distance

### Acoustic Model
The component of an ASR system that maps audio features to phonetic or linguistic units. Learns the relationship between sound patterns and speech sounds.

**Related Terms:** Language Model, End-to-End ASR, Phoneme Recognition

### Language Model (ASR)
A model that predicts the probability of word sequences, helping ASR choose among acoustically similar hypotheses. Domain-specific LMs dramatically improve accuracy.

**Related Terms:** Acoustic Model, N-gram, Neural Language Model, Perplexity

### Beam Search
A search algorithm that maintains multiple hypothesis candidates during decoding. Beam width controls the trade-off between thoroughness and speed.

**Related Terms:** Decoding, Greedy Search, Hypothesis, Pruning

### CTC (Connectionist Temporal Classification)
A loss function and decoding method that handles alignment between input sequences (audio frames) and output sequences without requiring explicit alignment labels.

**Related Terms:** Alignment, Loss Function, Blank Token, Decoding

### RNN-T (RNN Transducer)
An end-to-end ASR architecture combining encoder, prediction network, and joint network for streaming recognition. Dominant for on-device streaming ASR.

**Related Terms:** CTC, Transducer, Streaming ASR, End-to-End

### End-to-End ASR
ASR systems that directly map audio to text using a single neural network, without separate acoustic model, pronunciation dictionary, and language model.

**Related Terms:** Hybrid ASR, CTC, RNN-T, Attention-based ASR

### Streaming ASR
ASR that produces transcription results incrementally as audio arrives, rather than waiting for the complete utterance. Essential for interactive voice applications.

**Related Terms:** Real-Time ASR, Incremental Recognition, Online ASR

### Offline ASR
ASR that processes complete audio files after recording, with access to the full utterance including future context. Enables higher accuracy than streaming.

**Related Terms:** Batch Processing, Non-Streaming, Post-Processing

### OOV (Out-of-Vocabulary)
Words not in the ASR system's vocabulary or training data. Often misrecognized, particularly in traditional systems with fixed vocabularies.

**Related Terms:** Vocabulary, Unknown Word, Rare Word, Subword

### Confidence Score
A numerical estimate of how confident the ASR system is in its transcription, typically 0 to 1. Helps identify potentially erroneous transcriptions.

**Related Terms:** Posterior Probability, Uncertainty, Quality Estimation

### N-best List
A ranked list of the N most probable transcription hypotheses from ASR decoding. Provides alternatives when the top hypothesis may be incorrect.

**Related Terms:** Beam Search, Hypothesis, Alternative Transcription

### Punctuation Restoration
Adding punctuation marks to ASR output, which typically lacks punctuation. Essential for readability and downstream processing.

**Related Terms:** Truecasing, Post-Processing, Readability

### Inverse Text Normalization (ITN)
Converting spoken-form text to written-form (e.g., "one hundred twenty three dollars" to "$123"). Essential for human-readable transcripts.

**Related Terms:** Text Normalization, Formatting, Post-Processing

## Speech Synthesis

### Vocoder
A signal processing component that converts acoustic features (typically mel spectrograms) into audio waveforms. Quality vocoders (HiFi-GAN, WaveGlow) are critical for natural output.

**Related Terms:** Waveform Generation, HiFi-GAN, WaveNet, Griffin-Lim

### Prosody
The musical aspects of speech including intonation, stress, rhythm, and timing. Prosody conveys meaning, emotion, and emphasis beyond literal words.

**Related Terms:** Intonation, Stress, Rhythm, Expressiveness

### Fundamental Frequency (F0)
The lowest frequency of a periodic waveform, perceived as pitch. F0 varies over time to create intonation patterns that convey meaning and emotion.

**Related Terms:** Pitch, Intonation, Prosody, Voice Source

### Phoneme
The smallest unit of sound that distinguishes meaning in a language. English has approximately 44 phonemes represented by symbols like /p/, /ae/, /sh/.

**Related Terms:** Grapheme, Phonetics, Pronunciation, IPA

### Grapheme
The smallest unit of a writing system, typically a letter or character. Grapheme-to-phoneme (G2P) conversion maps written text to pronounced sounds.

**Related Terms:** Phoneme, G2P, Character, Letter

### G2P (Grapheme-to-Phoneme)
The process of converting written text (graphemes) into pronunciation (phonemes). Handles regular spelling patterns and exceptions.

**Related Terms:** Pronunciation Dictionary, Lexicon, Phoneme, Text Analysis

### Voice Cloning
Technology that creates a synthetic voice matching a specific person's voice characteristics from sample recordings. Modern systems can clone from minutes of audio.

**Related Terms:** Speaker Adaptation, Custom Voice, Voice Synthesis, Deepfake

### Speaker Adaptation
Techniques for adapting a TTS model to a new speaker's voice using limited data. Enables rapid custom voice creation without full retraining.

**Related Terms:** Voice Cloning, Transfer Learning, Fine-Tuning, Speaker Embedding

### Neural TTS
TTS systems using deep neural networks to generate natural-sounding speech. Has dramatically improved naturalness compared to older approaches.

**Related Terms:** Deep Learning TTS, End-to-End TTS, Tacotron, VITS

### Concatenative TTS
TTS approach that synthesizes speech by concatenating pre-recorded speech segments. Quality depends heavily on the unit database.

**Related Terms:** Unit Selection, Diphone, Speech Database, Legacy TTS

### SSML (Speech Synthesis Markup Language)
An XML-based markup language for controlling TTS output including pronunciation, prosody, volume, speaking rate, and pauses.

**Related Terms:** TTS Control, Pronunciation, Prosody Markup

### Formant
Resonant frequencies of the vocal tract that characterize vowel sounds. The first two formants (F1, F2) are primary cues for vowel identity.

**Related Terms:** Vocal Tract, Resonance, Vowel, Spectral Envelope

### Duration Model
A component that predicts how long each phoneme or linguistic unit should be spoken. Critical for natural rhythm and intelligibility.

**Related Terms:** Timing, Prosody, Rhythm, Speaking Rate

### Emotion Synthesis
TTS capability to generate speech expressing specific emotions (happy, sad, angry, excited). Varies prosody, speaking rate, and voice quality.

**Related Terms:** Expressive TTS, Prosody, Style Transfer, Affective Computing

## Conversation Management

### Turn-Taking
The process of managing when each participant speaks in a conversation. Effective turn-taking avoids awkward pauses and overlapping speech.

**Related Terms:** Endpointing, Barge-In, Dialogue Flow, Conversation Dynamics

### Endpointing
Detecting when a speaker has finished their turn, distinguishing final silence from mid-utterance pauses. Enables timely responses without interrupting users.

**Related Terms:** VAD, Turn-Taking, Silence Detection, Response Timing

### Barge-In
The capability for users to interrupt system speech and immediately begin a new command. Requires echo cancellation and speech recognition during playback.

**Related Terms:** Echo Cancellation, Interruption, Full Duplex, User Control

### Dialogue State
A structured representation of the current conversation context, including gathered information (slots), history, and system beliefs about user goals.

**Related Terms:** State Tracking, Context, Slot Filling, Conversation Memory

### Intent
The goal or action a user wants to accomplish with their utterance. Intent classification maps input to predefined action categories.

**Related Terms:** Intent Classification, User Goal, Action, NLU

### Slot
A parameter or piece of information needed to fulfill a user's intent. Slots have types (date, location, name) and may be required or optional.

**Related Terms:** Entity, Parameter, Slot Filling, Named Entity Recognition

### Entity
A specific piece of information extracted from user input, such as names, dates, locations, or product identifiers. Entities fill slots and parameterize actions.

**Related Terms:** NER, Slot, Named Entity Recognition, Information Extraction

### Context
The accumulated information from previous turns that influences interpretation of current utterances. Enables pronoun resolution and coherent multi-turn interactions.

**Related Terms:** Dialogue State, History, Coreference, Anaphora Resolution

### Prompt (Dialogue)
The initial instructions or context provided to an AI system that shapes its behavior, personality, and response style.

**Related Terms:** System Message, Instructions, Persona, Behavior Configuration

### Fallback
A response used when the system cannot understand or handle user input. Effective fallbacks offer specific guidance rather than generic messages.

**Related Terms:** Error Handling, Disambiguation, Clarification, Recovery

### Clarification
A system response asking the user to provide additional information or confirm understanding. Resolves ambiguity and fills missing slots.

**Related Terms:** Disambiguation, Confirmation, Slot Filling, Error Recovery

### Confirmation
Asking users to verify that the system correctly understood their request before taking action. Important for high-stakes or irreversible actions.

**Related Terms:** Verification, Acknowledgment, Clarification, Safety

### Disambiguation
Resolving ambiguous user input when multiple interpretations are possible. May involve clarifying questions or using context.

**Related Terms:** Clarification, Context, Interpretation, Ambiguity Resolution

### Dialogue Policy
The decision-making component that determines system actions based on dialogue state. Decides when to ask questions, provide information, or execute actions.

**Related Terms:** Dialogue Management, Decision Making, State Machine, Reinforcement Learning

### Conversation Flow
The designed sequence of interactions for accomplishing tasks, including prompts, expected responses, and branching logic.

**Related Terms:** Dialogue Design, User Journey, Interaction Design, VUI Design

### Repair
Strategies for recovering from misunderstandings or errors in conversation. Includes self-correction, user correction, and graceful error handling.

**Related Terms:** Error Recovery, Correction, Clarification, Fallback

## Telephony

### SIP (Session Initiation Protocol)
A signaling protocol for initiating, maintaining, and terminating real-time communication sessions. Foundation of VoIP telephony.

**Related Terms:** VoIP, PBX, Trunk, Call Control

### WebRTC
A set of standards enabling peer-to-peer audio/video communication directly in web browsers without plugins.

**Related Terms:** Browser Communication, Peer-to-Peer, Real-Time Media

### PSTN (Public Switched Telephone Network)
The traditional circuit-switched telephone network including landlines and cellular voice. PSTN audio has limited bandwidth (8kHz).

**Related Terms:** Telephony, Circuit-Switched, Phone Network, POTS

### DTMF (Dual-Tone Multi-Frequency)
The signaling tones generated when pressing telephone keypad buttons. Enables fallback input when voice recognition fails.

**Related Terms:** Touch-Tone, Keypad Input, IVR Navigation

### IVR (Interactive Voice Response)
An automated telephony system that interacts with callers through voice prompts and DTMF or speech input. Routes calls and handles simple transactions.

**Related Terms:** Voice Menu, Auto Attendant, Phone Bot, Call Automation

### SBC (Session Border Controller)
A network device that manages VoIP communication across network boundaries, providing security and quality of service.

**Related Terms:** Network Security, VoIP Gateway, Firewall, Border Security

### PBX (Private Branch Exchange)
A private telephone switching system serving an organization, connecting internal extensions and managing external call routing.

**Related Terms:** Phone System, IP-PBX, Unified Communications, Enterprise Telephony

### VoIP (Voice over IP)
Technology for delivering voice communications over IP networks instead of traditional circuit-switched networks.

**Related Terms:** Internet Telephony, SIP, IP Communications

### Trunk
A virtual connection between an organization and a telephony provider for making and receiving calls. SIP trunks replace physical phone lines.

**Related Terms:** SIP, Carrier, Phone Numbers, Connectivity

### DID (Direct Inward Dialing)
A service that provides individual phone numbers that route directly to specific destinations without going through a menu.

**Related Terms:** Phone Number, Inbound Calling, Number Provisioning

### Call Transfer
Moving an active call from one destination to another. Can be blind (immediate) or warm (with introduction).

**Related Terms:** Handoff, Escalation, Agent Transfer, Context Passing

### Call Recording
Capturing audio from telephone calls for quality assurance, compliance, or analytics. Subject to legal consent requirements.

**Related Terms:** Recording Compliance, Quality Assurance, Call Analytics

### Carrier
A company that provides telephone services and connectivity. Operates networks and offers SIP trunk services.

**Related Terms:** Telco, Provider, SIP Trunk, Connectivity

### G.711
A standard ITU audio codec for telephony, encoding at 64 kbps with 8kHz sampling. Universally supported but limited quality.

**Related Terms:** Codec, PCMU, PCMA, Telephony Audio

### Opus
A versatile, open audio codec supporting 6-510 kbps with excellent quality. Preferred for WebRTC and modern VoIP.

**Related Terms:** WebRTC, VoIP Codec, Variable Bitrate, Audio Compression

## Performance Metrics

### Latency
The time delay between an event and the system's response. Conversational AI targets under 500ms; >1000ms feels noticeably slow.

**Related Terms:** Response Time, Delay, TTFB, Processing Time

### TTFT (Time to First Token)
The latency from receiving input to generating the first output token. Determines perceived responsiveness in streaming responses.

**Related Terms:** First Byte Latency, Response Latency, Streaming

### TTFA (Time to First Audio)
The latency from user speech completion to the first synthesized audio output. Primary responsiveness metric for voice AI.

**Related Terms:** Response Latency, TTFT, Audio Latency

### RTF (Real-Time Factor)
The ratio of processing time to audio duration. RTF = 0.5 means 1 second of audio is processed in 0.5 seconds. RTF < 1.0 required for real-time.

**Related Terms:** Processing Speed, Throughput, Efficiency

### MOS (Mean Opinion Score)
A measure of audio quality based on human perception, rated 1-5. TTS scores range from ~3.0 (older) to ~4.5 (best neural). Human speech: 4.5-5.0.

**Related Terms:** Audio Quality, Naturalness, PESQ, POLQA

### Accuracy
The percentage of correct predictions. For ASR: 100% - WER. For intent classification: correct predictions / total.

**Related Terms:** WER, Precision, Recall, F1 Score

### Precision
The proportion of positive predictions that are actually correct. High precision means few false alarms.

**Related Terms:** Recall, F1 Score, False Positive Rate

### Recall
The proportion of actual positives correctly identified. High recall means few missed detections.

**Related Terms:** Precision, F1 Score, False Negative Rate

### F1 Score
The harmonic mean of precision and recall. F1 = 2 * (Precision * Recall) / (Precision + Recall).

**Related Terms:** Precision, Recall, Balanced Accuracy

### Throughput
The number of requests or operations processed per unit time. Measures concurrent sessions, utterances per second, or audio hours processed.

**Related Terms:** Concurrency, Capacity, Scalability, QPS

### P99 Latency
The latency threshold that 99% of requests complete within. Captures tail latency affecting worst-case experience.

**Related Terms:** Tail Latency, Percentile, SLA, Response Time Distribution

### Uptime
The percentage of time a system is operational. Expressed in "nines": 99.9% (three nines) = 8.76 hours downtime/year.

**Related Terms:** Availability, Reliability, SLA, Downtime

### Task Completion Rate
The percentage of user sessions that successfully accomplish their intended goal. The ultimate success metric.

**Related Terms:** Success Rate, Goal Completion, Containment Rate

## Infrastructure

### Streaming
Continuous transmission of data as it's generated rather than waiting for complete batches. Enables real-time voice processing.

**Related Terms:** Real-Time Processing, Chunked Transfer, WebSocket

### WebSocket
A communication protocol providing full-duplex channels over a single TCP connection. Standard for voice AI APIs.

**Related Terms:** Real-Time Communication, Bidirectional, Persistent Connection

### gRPC
A high-performance RPC framework using Protocol Buffers and HTTP/2. Suited for high-throughput, low-latency speech processing.

**Related Terms:** RPC, Protocol Buffers, HTTP/2, API

### REST API
An architectural style for web APIs using HTTP methods and stateless operations. Used for non-streaming voice operations.

**Related Terms:** HTTP API, Web Service, JSON, Endpoint

### Edge Computing
Processing data near its source rather than in centralized data centers. Reduces latency for voice AI on local devices.

**Related Terms:** On-Device Processing, Local Processing, Fog Computing

### GPU Inference
Using graphics processing units to accelerate neural network inference. Provides order-of-magnitude speedups over CPU.

**Related Terms:** Hardware Acceleration, CUDA, Inference Optimization, Tensor Cores

### TPU (Tensor Processing Unit)
Google's custom AI accelerator chip optimized for machine learning workloads. Powers Google's voice AI services.

**Related Terms:** AI Accelerator, Google Cloud, Custom Hardware

### Model Serving
Infrastructure for deploying trained models to handle inference requests at scale. Manages loading, routing, batching, and scaling.

**Related Terms:** Inference Server, Deployment, MLOps, Serving Infrastructure

### Batching
Combining multiple inference requests into a single batch for efficient GPU utilization. Trades latency for throughput.

**Related Terms:** Throughput Optimization, GPU Utilization, Latency Trade-off

### Load Balancing
Distributing incoming requests across multiple servers to ensure no single server is overwhelmed.

**Related Terms:** Horizontal Scaling, High Availability, Traffic Distribution

### Auto-Scaling
Automatically adjusting running instances based on demand. Adds capacity during peaks, removes during lulls.

**Related Terms:** Elasticity, Dynamic Scaling, Cloud Infrastructure

### Containerization
Packaging applications with dependencies into isolated containers (e.g., Docker) for consistent deployment.

**Related Terms:** Docker, Kubernetes, Microservices, Deployment

### Kubernetes
An open-source container orchestration platform for automating deployment, scaling, and management.

**Related Terms:** Container Orchestration, K8s, Cloud Native, Infrastructure

### Microservices
An architectural approach with small, independent services communicating via APIs. Enables independent scaling of ASR, NLU, TTS.

**Related Terms:** Service-Oriented Architecture, API-First, Distributed Systems

### CDN (Content Delivery Network)
A distributed network delivering content from locations close to users. Can cache pre-generated TTS audio.

**Related Terms:** Edge Caching, Geographic Distribution, Latency Reduction

## Business Terms

### Containment Rate
The percentage of interactions fully handled by voice AI without human assistance. Primary ROI metric. Rates: 20% (complex) to 80%+ (simple).

**Related Terms:** Automation Rate, Deflection Rate, Self-Service Rate

### AHT (Average Handle Time)
The average duration of customer interactions including talk time and after-call work. Voice AI aims to reduce AHT.

**Related Terms:** Handle Time, Call Duration, Efficiency

### FCR (First Contact Resolution)
The percentage of issues resolved during the first interaction without follow-up. Indicates resolution effectiveness.

**Related Terms:** Resolution Rate, First Call Resolution, Customer Effort

### CSAT (Customer Satisfaction)
A metric measuring satisfaction with an interaction, typically via post-call survey on a 1-5 or 1-10 scale.

**Related Terms:** NPS, Customer Experience, Survey Metrics

### NPS (Net Promoter Score)
A loyalty metric based on likelihood to recommend. NPS = % Promoters (9-10) - % Detractors (0-6). Range: -100 to +100.

**Related Terms:** CSAT, Customer Loyalty, Brand Perception

### CX (Customer Experience)
The overall perception customers have of interactions across all touchpoints. Voice AI is often a significant CX touchpoint.

**Related Terms:** User Experience, Customer Journey, Touchpoint

### ROI (Return on Investment)
A financial metric measuring profitability. ROI = (Net Profit / Cost) * 100%. Voice AI ROI comes from labor reduction and extended hours.

**Related Terms:** Cost Savings, Business Case, TCO

### TCO (Total Cost of Ownership)
Complete cost of implementing and operating a system over its lifetime, including direct costs, training, and maintenance.

**Related Terms:** ROI, Implementation Cost, Operating Cost

### SLA (Service Level Agreement)
A contract defining expected service quality metrics including uptime, latency, and support response times.

**Related Terms:** Uptime, Availability, Support, Contract

### ASA (Average Speed of Answer)
The average time callers wait before their call is answered. Voice AI can dramatically reduce ASA with instant response.

**Related Terms:** Wait Time, Queue Time, Abandonment Rate

### Abandonment Rate
The percentage of callers who hang up before completing their task. Voice AI reduces abandonment by eliminating wait times.

**Related Terms:** Drop Rate, ASA, Customer Experience

### Self-Service
Enabling customers to complete tasks independently without agent assistance. Voice AI extends self-service to voice channels.

**Related Terms:** Automation, Containment, IVR, Digital Self-Service

### Agent Assist
AI technology supporting human agents during interactions with relevant information, suggested responses, and automated work.

**Related Terms:** Agent Augmentation, Co-Pilot, Real-Time Guidance

### Escalation
Transferring an interaction from voice AI to a human agent when automation cannot resolve the issue.

**Related Terms:** Transfer, Handoff, Agent Routing, Fallback

### Call Volume
The number of calls received over a given period. Informs capacity planning and ROI calculations.

**Related Terms:** Traffic, Capacity, Peak Volume, Concurrency

### Intent Recognition Rate
The percentage of utterances where the system correctly identifies the intended action. Above 90% typically required for production.

**Related Terms:** NLU Accuracy, Classification Accuracy, Intent Detection

### Voice Commerce
Conducting commercial transactions through voice interfaces, including product search, ordering, and payment.

**Related Terms:** Conversational Commerce, Voice Shopping, Voice Payments

### Sentiment Analysis
Automated detection of emotional tone and attitude in speech, typically classifying as positive, negative, or neutral.

**Related Terms:** Emotion Detection, Tone Analysis, Customer Mood
