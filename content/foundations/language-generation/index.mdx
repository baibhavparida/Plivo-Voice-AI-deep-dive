# Language Generation (NLG)

Natural Language Generation (NLG) is the technology that creates human-like text responses in voice AI systems. NLG determines what your AI says and how it says it, directly impacting the quality of conversations.

## Why Language Generation Matters

The words your AI chooses shape the entire user experience:

- **Clarity**: Responses must be understood on first listen (no visual context)
- **Naturalness**: Generated text should sound conversational when spoken
- **Consistency**: Maintain a consistent persona and tone across interactions
- **Conciseness**: Voice responses need to be brief yet complete

## Topics in This Section

### [Response Templates](/topics/foundations/language-generation/response-templates)
Template-based response generation with variable substitution and conditional logic. Predictable, controlled responses for specific scenarios.

### [Dynamic Generation](/topics/foundations/language-generation/dynamic-generation)
LLM-powered dynamic response generation for flexible, contextual conversations. Leverage models like GPT-4 and Claude for natural dialogue.

### [Persona Consistency](/topics/foundations/language-generation/persona-consistency)
Maintaining consistent voice, tone, and personality across all agent responses. Build a coherent brand voice for your AI.

## Key Concepts

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Response Template** | Pre-defined response patterns with variable slots |
| **Dynamic Generation** | Real-time response creation using LLMs |
| **Persona** | The personality and character of your voice AI |
| **Tone** | The emotional quality of responses (friendly, professional, etc.) |
| **Verbosity** | The length and detail level of responses |

## Voice-Specific Considerations

NLG for voice differs from text generation:

- **No Visual Scanning**: Users can't skim; every word is heard sequentially
- **Memory Limits**: Listeners have limited working memory
- **Pacing**: Need natural pauses and rhythm for comprehension
- **Confirmation**: May need to repeat or confirm important information

## Template vs Dynamic Generation

| Approach | Best For | Trade-offs |
|----------|----------|------------|
| **Templates** | Compliance-critical, predictable flows | Limited flexibility |
| **Dynamic (LLM)** | Open-ended conversations, complex queries | Harder to control |
| **Hybrid** | Most production systems | Added complexity |
